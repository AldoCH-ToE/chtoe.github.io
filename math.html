Mathematical Framework

At the core of CH-ToE lies a principle that binds uncertainty, structure, and emergence: reality progresses through structured entropy reduction.

Definition of Knowledge

In CH-ToE, knowledge is defined mathematically as:

\( K = -\Delta S \)

Where:

  • \( \Delta S = S(t_1) - S(t_2) \)
  • \( S(t) \) is the system's entropy at time \( t \)

This means knowledge corresponds to a measurable decrease in entropy between two temporally adjacent system states.

Lambda Derivation

The theory introduces a universal transition threshold:

\( \lambda = \frac{\sqrt{8}}{\varphi} \approx 1.748 \)

Where \( \varphi \) is the golden ratio:

\( \varphi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2} \approx 1.618 \)

This value of \( \lambda \) emerges naturally from recursive geometric compression and harmonic structuring. It represents the cadence at which entropy reduction becomes self-sustaining and structure-generating.

Phase Transition Criteria

Systems reach a critical structuring point when the ratio of their entropy reduction to their prior uncertainty approaches λ. Formally:

\[ \text{If} \quad \frac{-\Delta S}{S(t_1)} \rightarrow \lambda, \quad \text{then a phase transition to knowledge occurs.} \]

The Hamiltonian of Knowledge

CH-ToE introduces a knowledge Hamiltonian to describe energy-like structuring dynamics:

\[ \mathcal{H}_K = -\lambda \cdot \frac{dS}{dt} \]

This formulation treats knowledge as a conserved, quantizable flow through entropy gradients — a dynamic sculpting of the phase space of reality.

Buk Units

Each discrete unit of structured entropy reduction is termed a Buk (Basic Unit of Knowledge). Buks are domain-specific but follow the same structuring principle. The total knowledge in a system at a given time is:

\[ K_{\text{total}} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \text{Buk}_i \]

Mathematical Summary

  • Knowledge: \( K = -\Delta S \)
  • Lambda: \( \lambda = \frac{\sqrt{8}}{\varphi} \approx 1.748 \)
  • Knowledge Hamiltonian: \( \mathcal{H}_K = -\lambda \cdot \frac{dS}{dt} \)
  • Phase Condition: \( \frac{-\Delta S}{S(t)} \rightarrow \lambda \)
  • Buk Total: \( K = \sum \text{Buk}_i \)

These relations form the backbone of CH-ToE’s formalism and allow falsifiable, predictive modeling across physical, biological, and artificial systems.
